Decentralized Waste Management for Modern Communities.

SudanWasta addresses the lack of structured waste collection in low-resource urban and peri-urban areas. We introduce a community-based system that collects, sorts, and converts local waste into valuable, high-utility products.

Systemic Failure

Lack of consistent, formal urban waste collection services in local neighborhoods.

Health Hazards

Environmental risks from plastic burning and drainage clogging, leading to disease.

Resource Scarcity

High costs of imported or traditional building materials for local growth.

Process Flow

The lifecycle of SudanWasta's decentralized system.

Waste Collection

Households and small businesses subscribe to a low-cost service. Waste is collected using carts or small vehicles by dedicated local operators.

Sorting & Segregation

Waste is transported to a small local hub where it is manually sorted into organic, plastic, and other recyclable categories.

Recycling & Conversion

Plastics are processed into durable products such as bricks or tiles. Organic waste is slated for conversion into compost in a future phase.

Sales & Distribution

Recycled products are distributed and sold to local construction markets or community partners.

Continuous Loop

The system creates a sustainable circular economy where waste is no longer discarded but transformed into a vital local resource.
